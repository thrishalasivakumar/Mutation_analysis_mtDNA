What is Mitochondrial DNA?

Mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA) is a small, circular strand of DNA found in the mitochondria, the energy-producing parts of the cell. Unlike most DNA, which is located in the cell‚Äôs nucleus, mtDNA exists outside the nucleus and is inherited exclusively from the mother.

Although it contains far fewer genes than nuclear DNA, mtDNA plays a vital role in the production of proteins and enzymes that help generate energy. Its unique location and inheritance pattern make it especially important in the study of genetic disorders, maternal ancestry, and human evolution.
